About

Who is Dana Ter?

Based mostly in Taipei, I write stories for in-flight magazines of airlines including EVA Air, Singapore Airlines, SilkAir, Scoot, Philippine Airlines, Hong Kong Airlines and UNI Air. My work is also published in ebookers, Marriott Traveler, DestinAsian, Roads & Kingdoms, Slate, Time Out Hong Kong and Travel in Taiwan, the official magazine of Taiwan's Tourism Bureau. I've filed stories from Taiwan, South Korea, Hong Kong, Japan, Cambodia, Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Myanmar, Guam, Australia, Portugal, London and New York City, and interviewed politicians, surfers, fortune tellers, farmers, celebrity chefs, cat breeders and fashion designers.

I'm currently the Taipei Correspondent for Louis Vuitton City Guides, published by BETC in Paris. My work involves writing and editing the second edition print guide, to be published in 2020, and updating the digital guide, which is available as an iPhone app. I cover hotels, restaurants, bars, fashion boutiques and art galleries, in addition to fact-checking and editing exisiting entries. From 2014-2017, I was a staff reporter and editor for the Features section of the English-language daily newspaper, the Taipei Times, where I started a column about Taiwan's craft beer and profiled local brewers (and sampled their beer!), and as the newspaper's art critic, wrote a weekly column about art exhibitions and fairs. Before that, I was an entertainment news columnist for Mic in New York City.

I have a Dual Master's degree in International and World History from Columbia University and the London School of Economics and am a surfer and licensed PADI Open Water Diver. I'm descended from generations of Peranakan sailors from Penang on my mom's side, while my dad's family is from Kuala Lumpur. In 1990, my parents left Malaysia for what was supposed to be a three-year stint in Hong Kong and they haven't stopped traveling since. I speak Mandarin and some Malay and spent most of my teenage years in a pine forest on Yangmingshan, a volcano in Taipei's outskirts. Growing up, I attended American schools in Malaysia, Hong Kong, Indonesia, Singapore and Taiwan and have also lived in Australia, Thailand, South Korea, the UK, the US and Sweden.
