About

Who is Dana Ter?

I was born and raised as a Third Culture Kid in an expat family that has been on the move for the last 26 years. I attended international schools across Asia and have lived in Malaysia, Indonesia, Singapore, Thailand, Hong Kong, Taiwan, South Korea, Australia, the UK, and the US.

Currently, I work as a journalist for the Taipei Times in Taipei, Taiwan, covering the arts and culture scene. This includes writing weekly art exhibition listings, restaurant reviews, and previews of events and festivals. I've also covered Aboriginal issues, political activism, and travel and surf stories around Taiwan. Before moving to Taipei, I worked as a freelance journalist in New York City, writing about arts and entertainment for publications like Mic and Contently.

Some of the more bizarre stories I’ve covered include Hello Kitty airplanes, couple t-shirts, hipster activist cafes, the ‘elitism’ of travel writing, and surfing on an island where no one knows how to swim.

I also have a Dual MA/MSc in international and world history from Columbia University and the London School of Economics.

2015-07-12, By Dana Ter
